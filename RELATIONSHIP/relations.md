### Core Concepts and Sample Sentences

- **Greeting and Basic Conversation:**
  - “Hello, how are you?” → *Namaste, main theek hoon, dhanyavaad. Aap kaise hain?*  
    (Hello, I am fine, thank you. How are you?)
  
- **Asking about Father:**
  - “Where is your father?” → *Aapke pita ji kahaan hain?*  
    (Father is a respectful term here, indicating respect for the father.)
  - Response: “My father is at home.” → *Mere pita ghar par hain.*

- **Talking about Siblings:**
  - “How many brothers and sisters do you have?” → *Aapke kitne bhai-behen hain?*
  - Example answers:
    - “I have two brothers.” → *Mere do bhai hain.*
    - “I have no sisters.” → *Meri koi behen nahi hai.*

- **Other Family Members:**
  - “Are there any other members in your family?” → *Kya aapke parivaar mein aur koi hai?*
  - Example: “My grandfather and grandmother stay with us.” → *Mere dada-dadi hamare saath rehte hain.*

- **Asking about Home Location:**
  - “Where is your house?” → *Aapka ghar kahaan hai?*
  - Example answer: “My house is in Patna.” → *Mera ghar Patna mein hai.*

---

### Important Notes on Usage and Grammar

- The word **"Pa"** or **"Pita"** is used for father, showing respect.
- The phrase **"kahaan"** means "where."
- The suffix **"-ji"** can be added to family member names (like *pita ji*) to show respect.
- **"Mere"** means "my" (for masculine nouns), and **"Meri"** is "my" (for feminine nouns).
- The term **"saath"** means "with," used here to indicate cohabitation or presence together.
- **"Par"** or **"mein"** denotes location—“at” or “in” a place.
- "Bhai" and "Behen" are key words for siblings.
- “Parivaar” stands for the family as a whole.
